🔐 What is Harvest-Now, Decrypt-Later?

Adversaries are collecting encrypted network traffic today. Once a cryptographically-relevant quantum computer (CRQC) exists, they replay stored ciphertext and decrypt it. Data encrypted with RSA, ECDH, or ECDSA is already at risk — even though the breach hasn't happened yet.

📄 NIST PQC Standards (August 2024)

FIPS 203 (ML-KEM) — replaces RSA/ECDH for key exchange.
FIPS 204 (ML-DSA) — replaces RSA/ECDSA for digital signatures.
FIPS 205 (SLH-DSA) — hash-based signature alternative (different math from ML-DSA).

📋 Compliance vs. Exposure

These are distinct questions. Compliance asks whether your algorithms match what NIST mandates. HNDL Exposure asks how much harvested ciphertext is at risk before a CRQC arrives — determined by algorithm vulnerability × data sensitivity × retention requirements.
